Question: What is AWS's operating income, and how is it reported in Amazon's financial statements?

## AWS Operating Income Overview

Amazon Web Services (AWS) is reported as a distinct segment within Amazon's financial statements. For **2022**, AWS operating income was **$22.8 billion**, and for **2023**, it increased to **$24.6 billion** [4]. This growth was driven by **increased sales** (primarily from higher customer usage) and **favorable foreign exchange rate impacts** of **$220 million** in 2023 [4]. 

### Key Drivers and Reporting Context
- **Sales Growth**: AWS sales rose **13% in 2023** compared to 2022, though this was partially offset by higher payroll, technology infrastructure costs, and pricing adjustments in long-term contracts [4].
- **Segment Reporting**: AWS operating income is disclosed in Amazon's consolidated statements of operations under the **"AWS"** segment. The segment's performance is separately highlighted in tables detailing operating income by region and business segment [4].
- **Foreign Exchange Impact**: Currency fluctuations positively affected AWS operating income in 2023, reflecting the segment's global operations and exposure to multiple currencies [4].

### Financial Statement Presentation
AWS operating income is included in Amazon's **consolidated operating income** alongside other segments (North America, International). The segment's results are critical to Amazon's overall profitability, contributing significantly to the **$36.9 billion consolidated operating income** reported for 2023 [4].
